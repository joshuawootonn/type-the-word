Hey there Reader,

It's Josh, creator of [Type the Word](https://www.typetheword.site/). I'm emailing you because you signed up for TTW, and I am starting a newsletter to keep people informed about TTW as it changes.

I created Type the Word last fall for an easier way to type through the Bible. After launching it, I took some time off the project, but received many kind emails thanking me for it and usage has been growing month to month.

28,936 verses have been typed, which means as a group we have almost typed through an entire Bible worth of verses.

Your discipline in typing through the Bible has been so motivating to me. So I have been working on the project again, and I am starting this newsletter to keep you up to date.

## July Update

### Created a Changelog notice

The changelog is another way I'll be communicating with you all. It's a more immediate way to see what is happening if you are curious.

I created a twinkle notice above the changelog link in the footer to indicate when there are changelog updates you haven't seen. Visiting the [/changelog](https://www.typetheword.site/changelog) page dismisses this notice.

### Updated TTW to remember where you last typed

TTW now remembers where you last typed. When you are logged in and load the [home page](https://www.typetheword.site/) (`/`) TTW redirects you to the last location you typed.

You can also navigate to your last typing location by clicking the TTW logo in the header.

### Migrated to App Router (Programming Update)

Another big change this past month was migrating to App Router.

Originally, I built Type the Word on some new tech so that I could try TRPC and Axiom. Now that it's growing, I have migrated to more stable platforms to lower the maintenance burden.

- Type the Word is open source. If you are ever curious to see how it works, check out the [Github repo here](https://github.com/joshuawootonn/type-the-word).
- You may have noticed some short outages around this time. Sorry for the disruption. I don't think there will be [this big](https://github.com/joshuawootonn/type-the-word/pull/7) of an architectural change again.
